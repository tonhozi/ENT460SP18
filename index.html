Course Overview:

Credit Hours: 3 | ENT 460 – LEC 0001

This is a capstone course that looks at processes and skills needed to create a new enterprise. You will learn how to take a business idea, assess the idea, develop an appropriate business model and turn that model into a business plan that will be useful for both internal and external audiences. This course will also orient you to the uncertainties and stresses that are prevalent in startup situations. Prerequisites include ACCTG 211, FIN 325, MKT 224, MGMT 330 and DSOM 326.

You and your team members will come up with a venture idea that solves a particular problem and thereby add value to you, the entrepreneur and your venture’s other stakeholders – the customers, suppliers, employees, investors and community. We will define entrepreneurial ventures broadly incorporating both for profit and nonprofit scenarios.

A key vehicle for the entrepreneur’s effort is the business plan. In most cases multiple versions of it are required as the opportunity and business change overtime. The plan helps the entrepreneur attract support and resources from other stakeholders because it tells them what the business is about, what its strategy will be, how its management thinks and what the financial risks and rewards most likely will be. It also helps the entrepreneur to manage a growing and necessarily complex set of dynamics by providing mileposts and indicating the resources that will be necessary to achieve them. Finally, it provides a set of standards against which actual performance can be compared. A great plan by itself is no guarantee of success. A successful startup requires relentless execution and implementation against the plan and flexibility in reevaluating and changing the plan. The ultimate validations are customers’ orders and a sustainable venture that creates value for stakeholders.

Course Objectives:

· Primary: Learn how to formally plan for a business Launch: The process includes identifying a business opportunity – assessing the risks and rewards and formally planning for a business launch. The students, working in a team will prepare a business plan – that transforms initial ideas into action plans.

· Secondary: Internalize the Entrepreneurial Mindset: Understand what it takes to be an entrepreneur and how the mindset helps one excel in both startup situations and also in corporate careers.

Individual Assignments (45%):

Following are short descriptions of assignments and expectations. I will send more detail guidelines as necessary when due time nears.

Class Participation & Attendance (5%+5%=10%): 5% of your total grades will be attendance. For you to benefit most from the course, you need to be present every class. If you must be absent for any reason, please notify me (by email) before class with appropriate reasons. More than 2 unexcused absences will result in loss of grade. It is important for you not only to present but also to prepare for each class session. Complete the assigned readings prior to class and you will better learn from comments of other students and the discussions. Your participation is class will be evaluated at the end. I will sort all the students in three categories based on my observations throughout the semester. 5% of your grades will be participation or contribution to class. Please ask me anytime if you need to know where you are.

Individual Business Idea (5%): This exercise is intended to get you thinking about the details of a business idea. The business idea page should not exceed one page, single-spaced, 1-inch margins, 12-point font, with student Name in the Header. The analysis will help you form an understanding of which idea might become an opportunity. You will also provide an elevator pitch (1 minute) in class.

Exam (10% +10%): Two in class written exams will be held during the semester. Questions will be in the form of short and multiple-choice variety and the exam will cover classes and discussions that take place before exam dates.

POP Quiz & Other Unannounced Assignments (5%): On some days a pop quiz may appear. Sometimes we will grade in class work. These will total 5% points.

Peer Assessment - Participation in Teamwork (5%): Each team member will assess other participant’s teamwork. The assessments will be collated to get to individual grades. One cannot assess him or herself. The grades will be tied to the quality of group work.

Team Assignments (55%):

In teams of 4-5 students, you will explore an opportunity to the point where you can create a full business plan. Each team assignment will build upon the previous one. All group members will receive the same grade for all assignments. There is an individual component on team contribution, which is explained under individual assignment section.

Team Venture Idea Memo (5%): Each team will submit a memo (up to 2 pages) introducing an opportunity and how each member will contribute to the group effort. Detail expectations will be sent out before assignment.

Team Business Model (10%): Each team will develop and submit a business model. The business model toolkit will be introduced in class. Further details will be forthcoming.

Marketing Plan (8%): Each team will submit (both by email and hard copy) a partial feasibility report/ business plan (single spaced – maximum 6 pages) that includes description of your venture on Market Research and Marketing Plan. The more detailed and substantive (based on data and figures) the market research and marketing plan the higher the grades will be. Detail expectations will be sent out before assignment.

Financial Plan (7%): Each team will submit (both by email and hard copy) a partial feasibility report/business plan (single spaced – maximum 6 pages) that includes description of your team venture on Financial Plan, Growth Plan and Contingency Plan. The more detail and substantive (based on logical assumptions) the plans the higher the grades will be. Detail expectations will be sent out before assignment.

VCC Submission & Performance (5%): Each year in Spring, The Bloch School organizes Regnier Venture Creation Challenge, which is a premier business planning competition in Kansas City area. Total prize money usually exceeds 45,000 dollars. As part of your team deliverable, your plan will be submitted to the competition. For participating you will get 4% of your grades. The rest 1% will depend on how you perform. Teams from previous classes have gone onto the final stage. The completion is in first half of May. I will share exact dates and deadlines in future classes.

Final Business Plan & Executive Summary (10%): Each team will submit a Business Plan compiling previous assignments incorporating feedback from both faculty and other mentors.

Team Presentation (10%): Each team will present their venture idea in front of class. (Approx. 6 minutes of presentation followed by 4 minutes of Q&A). Entrepreneurship is more than just having a great idea. It is ultimately about convincing others to come on board. In this spirit, think creatively and engagingly about your presentation. Again, I shall send out more detailed expectations prior to the assignment.
